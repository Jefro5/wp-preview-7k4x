How do I set the live wallpaper?

Tap the app's icon — it opens a wallpaper preview right away. On the preview screen, tap Set wallpaper and choose Home screen (or Home and lock screen). That's it — the app then opens its Settings so you can start customizing.

You can also get there the Android way: long-press an empty spot on your home screen → Wallpaper & style → find the app under live wallpapers.

How do I open the wallpaper's settings?

Tap the small floating settings button on your home screen wallpaper (you can move it, or hide it, from within Settings). You can also open the wallpaper preview and tap the gear icon in the corner.

Can I use my own photo?

Yes. Open Settings → CUSTOMIZE, turn on Custom Photo, then tap SET PHOTO and pick an image from your gallery. The photo becomes part of the scene.

If a photo won't set, it's almost always a very large image or one still downloading from an online source — try a photo already saved on the phone, or one straight from your camera.

How do I reset the photo back to the default?

In Settings → CUSTOMIZE, with Custom Photo on, tap RESET PHOTO. It restores the scene's original artwork in one tap — no reinstall needed.

I can't set my custom photo.

Almost always it's a very large image, or one that's still downloading from an online/cloud source. Pick a photo that's already saved on your phone — or snap a quick one with the camera — and try that; local images set instantly.

If a big image still won't take, reboot the phone once to clear its memory and try again.

What are Themes?

A Theme is a ready-made bundle of settings — a coordinated look you can apply in one tap from the THEMES section. Prefer your own mix? Just set the individual options and skip the themes; nothing is locked.

My wallpaper keeps changing scenes on its own.

That's the double-tap shortcut. When it's set to Change Scene, double-tapping the wallpaper switches scenes — handy, but easy to trigger by accident. Turn it off, or change what a double-tap does, under Settings → CUSTOMIZE → Double Click.

How does the weather work? Do you track my location?

No tracking. Live weather is off by default and entirely opt-in — and by default it needs no Android permission at all: it estimates your approximate city from your internet connection, or you can simply pick your city from a list.

For exact accuracy there's an optional “Local” choice that uses your phone's location. It only works if you select it yourself, Android asks for your permission first, it's city-level only (never GPS-precise), and it's used solely to fetch weather and compute sunrise/sunset — nothing is stored off your device or shared.

Forecast data comes from MET Norway, the Norwegian Meteorological Institute.

What do you do with my data?

The short version: nothing leaves your phone that you didn't ask for. There are no accounts, we don't collect personal data, and we never sell anything about you.

The only thing ever sent out is when you opt into live weather — then your approximate city (estimated from your connection, or picked by you) goes out to fetch a forecast, and nothing more. Full details are in the Privacy section below.

What permissions does the app need, and why?

As few as possible:

Internet — only for optional live weather and checking for updates.
Approximate location — only if you turn on the “Local” weather option; Android asks first, it's city-level, and it's never used in the background.
Notifications — only if you opt in to update notices.
Billing — for the one-time Remove Ads purchase.

Choosing your own photo uses Android's built-in photo picker, so it needs no photos or storage permission. No contacts, no precise or background location, no tracking.

The time of day or sky doesn't match what's outside.

By default the scene follows the real sun — sunrise, daytime, sunset and night — for your location. If it looks off, open Settings → CUSTOMIZE → TIME OF DAY and either pick your city, or choose Auto Local so it uses your exact sunrise and sunset times.

Want to set the mood yourself instead? Turn on Custom Sunrise & Sunset to dial in your own times, or pick a fixed time of day.

The wallpaper doesn't move when I swipe between home screens.

Some phone launchers (Samsung's default, among others) don't tell wallpapers about swiping. The app detects this and switches to Manual panning — touch and drag the wallpaper itself to pan it.

You can pick your preferred mode any time under Settings → DISPLAY → Camera Panning, and set how many home screens you have there too.

When I swipe, the view doesn't line up with my home screens.

On launchers that don't report swipes, the wallpaper uses Manual panning and can occasionally drift out of step with your pages. Set the exact number of home screens you have under Settings → DISPLAY → Camera Panning so it snaps to each page — and a quick pan back re-aligns it.

Will a live wallpaper drain my battery?

Far less than you'd expect. The wallpaper only renders while it's actually visible — the moment you open an app or the screen turns off, it stops doing work entirely.

You can check its usage yourself in System Settings → Battery — in normal use it sits low, typically well under a couple of percent.

If you want to trim it further, the PERFORMANCE tab in the wallpaper's settings lets you lower the visual quality and frame rate.

One note: your phone's battery screen lists a “screen on” figure next to the wallpaper — that's the cost of the display being on, not the wallpaper's own work.

The wallpaper feels slow or stutters.

The usual cause is something else on your home screen competing for the phone — busy widgets or a heavy custom launcher can starve the wallpaper of CPU. Try removing widgets one at a time to find the culprit.

You can also drop the load in Settings → PERFORMANCE: choose the Low or Medium quality tier for a lighter, smoother render.

Why are there ads?

Ads keep the apps free. They only ever appear inside the Settings screens — never on your wallpaper itself.

How do I remove the ads — and do I keep it on a new phone?

Open Settings → CUSTOMIZE and tap REMOVE ADS. It's a single one-time purchase — no subscription.

It's tied to your Google account, so it follows you: reinstall the app or set up a new phone with the same account and Remove Ads comes back automatically. There's no “restore” button to hunt for — just sign in with the same account.

I bought Remove Ads but the ads are still showing.

Make sure you're signed in with the same Google account you purchased with, on a solid connection, then fully close and reopen the app so it can re-check with Google Play. It restores automatically — there's no code to enter.

Still seeing ads after a reopen? Email us and we'll sort it out.

Is there any sound?

No — the wallpapers are silent by design, so they never interrupt your music or make noise in your pocket.

What languages are supported?

English for now. More languages are on the list — if there's one you'd love to see, drop us a note from the Support section below.

Which phones are supported?

Android 8.0 and newer. Live wallpapers aren't available on iOS, Fire tablets, or Fire TV — that's a platform limitation, not an app setting.

I get a black screen, or the wallpaper crashes.

This is nearly always the environment rather than the scene. The usual culprits and fixes:

• Custom ROM or third-party launcher — test on your phone's stock launcher to rule it out.
• Out-of-date Android — update via System Settings → System → System update.
• Very low free memory — close background apps and heavy widgets.

If it persists, the in-app EMAIL SUPPORT button attaches your device details so we can dig in.

How do I uninstall the wallpaper?

Set a different wallpaper first (some phones won't let you remove the one that's currently live), then uninstall the app the normal way: System Settings → Apps → the app → Uninstall. Or long-press its icon and choose Uninstall.

My payment was declined, or I'd like a refund.

Purchases and refunds are handled entirely by Google Play, not by us — so those are quickest to sort out on Google's side. The Google Play help links just below cover declined payments, order history and refunds.

If it's about an issue with the app itself, email us and we'll make it right.

The app won't download or install from Google Play.

Downloads are handled by Google Play, and the usual fix is to clear its cache: System Settings → Apps → Google Play Store → Storage → Clear cache (then Clear data if needed), then reboot and try again on a solid connection. The Google Play help links above cover the rest.

I found a bug or have a suggestion.

Please tell us — a lot of what ships comes straight from user feedback. The fastest route is the EMAIL SUPPORT button in the app (Settings → MORE), which attaches your app version and device details automatically, so we can get straight to it. Or email support@3dgaze.com.

Privacy

Privacy, in plain words

3DGaze wallpapers don't collect personal data and never sell anything about you. Optional live weather uses your approximate city — estimated from your internet connection, or picked by you from a list — solely to fetch a forecast. The optional “Local” mode uses your phone's coarse (city-level) location instead: Android asks for the location permission only if you turn it on, it's used only for weather and sunrise/sunset, and nothing is stored off your device or shared. Ads in the Settings screens are served by Unity Ads under their own policy. The full privacy policy will live on this page.
